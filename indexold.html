MOODBOARD & ISSUES

An analysis of the brands form language and an inspiration board describing what we want to add to it.

Lens is hard to change
When the weather conditions change you might need to change the lens. or you need to replace the lens when it’s damaged as most lenses are mounted inside the frame, and considering the cold weather and wear of gloves this can be quite tricky.

Goggle Straps Won’t Stay In Place
Goggle straps are constantly adjusted meaning the fit also change causing air draft and affecting ventilation. they are also quite slippery and are easily lost when trying to get a sun tan in the ski lift

Design Causes Water To Get Inside The typical - lens in a frame - goggle design means a lot of corners where snow gets stuck, melts and gets inside the goggle - and then fogs up the lens

Strap Attachment Limits Vision
The nature of a typical strap to helmet attachment design creates limitation in the field of view

Rigid Nose Arch Prevents Good Fitting and bad ventilation means fogging

Strap Holder Are Complicated To Use
Being located in the back, this is where the strap is fixed to the helmet - meaning either a lot of fiddling with you hands behind your head or taking the helmet off

SEMANTICS & FITTING

Semantics and helmet fitting

Helmet Fitting
The goggle has been designed to fit the whole range of pocs ski helmets, but an average of other brands helmet dimensions were used in the equation as well.

Goggle Strap Holder
located inside of the goggle frame provides a better fit meaning less air draught and fogging

Contrast
The google is designed to complement the helmets by contrasting in colors, graphics (sizes) and detail juxtapositions (see inspiration board)

Lens Change
To change lens - simply push on the frame above the lens lock buttons to release it

The Frame
The frame is made of injection molded pu enabling a construction that lets a lot of air through

The Lens
Polycarbonate lens with clean edges and easy to fit slots for lens holders

Main Air Intakes
Big intakes for good air circulation

Main Air Outlet
Big outlet letting out a lot of air

Goggle Strap Attachment
is offset inwards to provide better air flow

Side Outlets
provides even better circulation of air

DESIGN Process

This is a description of my general full design process.

As every project is different, the process is always customized to your project.

By supplying me with an as detailed as possible description of your idea or problem to be solved we have the best starting conditions for a great design project!

You might have rough sketches, mockups, color ideas, target group ideas and so on that can help describe what you are looking for.

Anything that can help me understand what you want to achieve is valueable and will be used later on in my design process!

Design Research

User Research

Who will be using the product?

User research focuses on understanding user behaviors, needs, and motivations through observation techniques, task analysis, and other feedback methodologies.

This field of research aims at improving the usability of products, services, or processes

Market Research

Here, we take a look at what products yours is competing with, where in the market there is room for it and what technology and solutions are used.

We look at products already available on the market, to see what should be improved.

This is done with a concept evaluation

Context Research

Now, we'll look at the environment where the product will be used.

Where do you put the product when not in use?
Will the surroundings be dark or light where the product will be used and how does that affect the design of the the product?

One could say that the context is the "second user" and strongly affect how a product is designed.
Analysing both existing products and mock-ups in its environment reveals issues that need to be solved for the product to function correctly.

Concept Ideation

In this stage of the process, it's time to start generating some ideas!

From the gathered information in the analyze phase, creative techniques such as brainstorming, quick sketching and other different lateral thinking methods are applied to generate ideas.

Mood Board

A mood board is a visual presentation or collage consisting of images, text, and samples of objects in a composition, meant to convey a general idea or feeling about a particular topic.
In our case, we use it as a language to describe what expression the product will have.

Is it supposed to express a safe feeling?
What does safe mean to you?

How we react to a products semantics is depending on culture, personal experiences and other factors, meaning that we need to communicate what we are trying to achieve with tools that say more than just words.

Concept Sketching

Next, we visualize our generated ideas with sketches.
Rough sketches are drawn to quickly communicate an idea.

By using a shared folder with notifications, you can immediately check out a new sketch once it's finished.

Modeling & Prototyping

Now, how do we know if this idea that we just came up with is any good?

Our next step in the process is to evaluate how our concept would work in the real world.

Normally, we start off by making a quick and rough foam model which is evaluated together with users from our target group.
This is an iterative process where the concept is further refined.

We then transfer our model to digital space to refine it even more, and to start creating a CAD model that can be 3d printed or sent off to the manufacturer.

In design we use our research and analysis from the earlier design process phases and break it down to functions.
In our user analysis, we might have encountered a problem with body injuries from a badly designed truck seat due to entering and exiting the truck cab.
We find that we must "facilitate exiting and entering"

Lateral Thinking is a deliberate, systematic creative-thinking process that deliberately looks at challenges from completely different angles.
By introducing specific, unconventional thinking techniques, lateral thinking enables thinkers to find novel solutions that would otherwise remain uncovered. Lateral thinking focuses on what could be rather than what is possible.

In Product Design a design concepts functionality is evaluated by studying the products interaction with the users and its surroundings.

Breaking down the interactions to functions, we then organize them in flowcharts that visualize issues that might not be immidiately visible to the named eye.
Some areas may then stand out as problematic, and it may be considered an area that needs to be refined or re-done
